> ਜਿਨ੍ਹਾਂ ਸਿੰਘਾਂ ਸਿੰਘਣੀਆਂ ਨੇ ਧਰਮ ਹੇਤ ਸੀਸ ਦਿੱਤੇ, ਬੰਦ ਬੰਦ ਕਟਾਏ, ਖੋਪਰੀਆਂ ਲੁਹਾਈਆਂ, ਚਰਖੜੀਆਂ ਤੇ ਚੜ੍ਹੇ, ਆਰਿਆਂ ਨਾਲ ਚਿਰਾਏ ਗਏ... ਧਰਮ ਨਹੀਂ ਹਾਰਿਆ ॥ — Ardas

Notes I keep on the shaheeds named (and unnamed) in Ardas. *Shaheed* means witness: one whose life — and, if demanded, death — testifies that there is something in a human being no empire can touch. The price of the path was set by Guru Nanak Dev Ji himself: ਜਉ ਤਉ ਪ੍ਰੇਮ ਖੇਲਣ ਕਾ ਚਾਉ ॥ ਸਿਰੁ ਧਰਿ ਤਲੀ ਗਲੀ ਮੇਰੀ ਆਉ ॥ — "if you wish to play the game of love, step onto my path with your head on your palm." ## The shaheed Gurus

- **Guru Arjan Dev Ji** — Lahore, 1606. Seated on the tatti tavi, burning sand poured over him, reciting ਤੇਰਾ ਕੀਆ ਮੀਠਾ ਲਾਗੈ ॥. Shaheedan de Sirtaj — the first shaheedi, after which the sixth Guru took up miri–piri.
- **Guru Tegh Bahadur Ji** — Chandni Chowk, Delhi, 1675. Gave his sis for the freedom of another people's tilak and janju. Hind di Chadar. Shaheed with him: **Bhai Mati Das** (sawn alive), **Bhai Dayala** (boiled alive), **Bhai Sati Das** (burned alive) — none wavered.

## The era of Guru Gobind Singh Ji

- **Sahibzada Ajit Singh** (17) and **Sahibzada Jujhar Singh** (13) — shaheed in battle at Chamkaur, Poh 1704, each sent into the field by their father.
- **Bhai Himmat Singh, Bhai Mohkam Singh, Bhai Sahib Singh** — three of the Panj Piare, shaheed at Chamkaur.
- **Bhai Jiwan Singh (Bhai Jaita)** — who carried the Ninth Guru's sis from Delhi to Anandpur; shaheed at Chamkaur. **Bhai Sangat Singh** — wore the Guru's kalgi in the garhi. **Bhai Bachittar Singh** — of the nagni barchha; fell after the Sarsa crossing.
- **Sahibzada Zorawar Singh** (8) and **Sahibzada Fateh Singh** (5) — bricked alive at Sirhind for refusing to abandon Sikhi; **Mata Gujri Ji** shaheed in the Thanda Burj the same day.
- **The Chali Mukte** — the forty of Khidrana (Muktsar), 1705, led back into battle by **Mai Bhago**, who survived the field; pardoned by the Guru as Bhai Maha Singh breathed his last.
- **Bibi Harsharan Kaur** — cremated the shaheeds of Chamkaur alone by night; burnt at the pyre she lit.

## The 18th century

- **1716 — Baba Banda Singh Bahadur and ~740 Singhs**, Delhi. Executed over days; offered pardon for conversion daily, not one accepted; his young son killed before his eyes.
- **1734 — Bhai Mani Singh**, Lahore. The Panth's great scholar-scribe, cut joint by joint (band band kataye).
- **1739 — Bhai Bota Singh & Bhai Garja Singh** — declared the Khalsa alive by levying toll on the royal road; fell fighting the detachment sent against them.
- **1745 — Bhai Taru Singh** — chose his scalp over his kesh.
- **1745 — Bhai Subeg Singh & Bhai Shahbaz Singh** — father and son, broken together on the charkhari.
- **1746 — Chhota Ghallughara**, Kahnuvan — thousands of Sikhs, many women and children.
- **1748–53 — the Singhniyan of Mir Mannu's jail** — ground sava-sava mann of grain daily; their children cut and hung in garlands around their necks; not one converted.
- **1757 — Baba Deep Singh** — kept the vow of Damdama; fell within sight of Harmandir Sahib.
- **1762 — Vadda Ghallughara**, Kup-Rahira — tens of thousands, near half the Panth, in one day. The Khalsa took Sirhind within the year.
- **1764 — Baba Gurbaksh Singh** — thirty Singhs before Sri Akal Takht Sahib against thirty thousand.

## Shaheedi pehra

*Pehra* is the sentry's watch. In the Panth's understanding it runs three ways:

- **The watch they stood** — every generation from 1606 onward drew the night shift of history and stood it, with no evidence the dawn would come.
- **The watch they still stand** — in puratan and Nihang tradition the shaheeds remain a guarding host over the Panth, remembered at every Ardas and shaheedi asthan.
- **The watch that rotates to us** — Ardas is a shift-change, not a memorial. The daily forms are small: kesh kept, bani read, someone else's freedom of conscience defended, the names taught to our children.

ਸ਼ਹੀਦਾਂ ਦੀ ਕਮਾਈ ਦਾ ਧਿਆਨ ਧਰ ਕੇ, ਬੋਲੋ ਜੀ ਵਾਹਿਗੁਰੂ ॥
