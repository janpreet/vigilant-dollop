Notes I keep as I learn my way around the puratan granths. One orientation first: **Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji alone is the Guru.** The granths below are the literature of the Guru's darbar and the Panth — the Tenth Master's bani, history, rahit, and prophecy around the throne of Gurbani. ## Sri Dasam Granth Sahib

- **What:** the bani of Guru Gobind Singh Ji — Braj, Punjabi and Persian in Gurmukhi; 1,428 angs in the standard sarup. Enthroned beside Guru Granth Sahib Ji at Takht Hazur Sahib, Takht Patna Sahib and in the Nihang dals.
- **Inside:** Jaap Sahib · Akal Ustat · Bachittar Natak (the Guru's own autobiography) · Chandi Charitras & Chandi di Var (the first pauri of Ardas) · Gyan Prabodh · Chaubis Avtar · Shabad Hazare · 33 Savaiye · Khalsa Mahima · Zafarnama.
- **You already recite it:** Jaap Sahib, Tav-Prasad Savaiye and Chaupai Sahib of nitnem, the amrit sanchar banis, and the opening of Ardas are all Dasam bani.
- **Start:** Jaap Sahib with a translation; then Bachittar Natak and the Zafarnama.

## Sri Sarabloh Granth

- **What:** the Granth of All-Steel (also called Manglacharan Puran) — Guru Gobind Singh Ji's granth from the darbar at Nanded; kept by the Buddha Dal and Takht Hazur Sahib. Five chapters; Chapter 1 alone is 753 verses.
- **Inside:** the war of the devas against the demon Bhimnad, framing Akal Purakh as sarab-loh — the deathless power behind all forms; shastar as a saroop of the Divine. Home of the Khalsa Mahima: ਖ਼ਾਲਸਾ ਮੇਰੋ ਰੂਪ ਹੈ ਖ਼ਾਸ ॥
- **Why it matters:** the theological heart of the Khalsa's warrior identity and of sarbloh bibek; the Buddha Dal includes it with Guru Granth Sahib and Dasam Granth in its akhand paths.
- **Start:** the Khalsa Mahima, then Chapter 1 with a satik.

## Sri Gur Partap Suraj Granth (Suraj Prakash)

- **What:** the lives of all ten Gurus and Baba Banda Singh Bahadur in ~51,800 verses — Kavi Santokh Singh, completed 1843. Structured as a poetic cosmos: 12 rasis, 6 ruts, 2 ayans; every chapter an ansu (sun-ray).
- **Why it matters:** the text of daily katha in gurdwaras worldwide, including Sri Harmandir Sahib. Nearly every sakhi we grew up hearing was routed through it.
- **Start:** don't read it — listen. Daily Suraj Prakash katha is on every platform; the Bhai Vir Singh 14-volume steek for readers.

## Prem Sumarag Granth

- **What:** "The True Path of Love" — the most complete rahitnama; 10 chapters of Punjabi prose, from personal rahit to statecraft.
- **Inside:** initiation, daily discipline, birth, naming, anand karaj, death; then economy, justice, and the model of a Khalsa state under Akal Purakh's sovereignty. Women receive pahul, girls are educated, widows remarry with honour.
- **Start:** the life-cycle chapters — readable in an afternoon, and they explain why Sikh rites of passage look the way they do.

## Sau Sakhi (Guru Ratan Mal)

- **What:** one hundred sakhis of Guru Gobind Singh Ji — his words narrated by Bhai Ram Kuar Ji of the house of Baba Buddha Ji (later Bhai Gurbakhsh Singh), recorded by Bhai Sahib Singh.
- **Inside:** episodes, rahit teachings, and the prophecies of the Khalsa's coming sovereignty — raj karega khalsa.
- **Why it matters:** hope in manuscript form through the darkest persecution; it fired the Namdhari movement under Baba Ram Singh, and the British monitored its prophecies.
- **Start:** after the others — the prophecies land with more force once you know the history.

## The gupt banis of the dals

Rare compositions preserved in the puratan sarups and the gutkas of the dals:

- **ਉਗ੍ਰਦੰਤੀ Ugardanti** — six chhands from the Patna Sahib sarup (compilation tradition of Baba Deep Singh, 1698); given by the Guru so the weak find strength against tyranny; in Nihang and Namdhari nitnem.
- **ਸ੍ਰੀ ਭਗੌਤੀ ਅਸਤੋਤ੍ਰ Sri Bhagauti Astotra** (Sri Sahib ji ki Ustati) — praise of the Divine-Sword, in the Buddha Dal's puratan Khalsa Nitnem gutkas.
- **ਬ੍ਰਹਮ ਕਵਚ Brahm Kavach** — the protective bani of the Sarbloh tradition, recited by Nihang Singhs before battle and in the dals' nitnem to this day.
- **ਆਰਤੀ ਆਰਤਾ Arati-Arata** — the full puratan evening prayer with Guru Gobind Singh Ji's verses, sung nightly at Hazur Sahib and in the dals.

## A path in

1. **Listen** — sakhis and Suraj Prakash katha; the tradition is oral first.
2. **Read short + translated** — Kavi Kankan's *Das Gur Katha*: all ten Gurus by a court poet, 120 pages.
3. **The Guru's voice** — Dasam Granth: Jaap Sahib, Bachittar Natak, Zafarnama.
4. **Live the code** — Prem Sumarag's life-cycle chapters.
5. **Go deep** — Sarabloh with a satik; the gupt banis.
6. **Destiny** — Sau Sakhi.
